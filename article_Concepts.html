Poker Concepts

Concepts

Expected Value

The expected value (EV) is a statistical measure used to determine the average outcome of a particular decision over the long run.

Equity

Equity refers to the share of the pot that belongs to a player if there were no more actions to be made and the rest of the cards are dealt. It is based on the probability that a player will have the best hand after the cards are dealt.

Landing equity

The landing equity of your hand is of your hand's equity before your opponent has reacted to your bet.

Finishing equity

The finishing equity of your hand is your hand's equity after your opponent has called your bet.

Value Bet

Bet made with a strong hand that expects to build a pot against a range that it will be a head of.

Bluff

Bluffing means betting with a hand that, when your opponent folds, he will frequently be folding hands with high equity against your hand.

Equity Denial

Equity denial is when you prevent a player from realizing his equity by forcing him to fold before showdown.

Urgency

Urgency refers to how imporant it is to value bet Now. It is dependant on the amount of time remaing in a hand to invest money compared with the average investment ceiling your hand wants to reach.

Minimum Defense Frenquency (MDF)

Minimum defense frequency (MDF) describes the portion of your range that you must continue with when facing a bet in order to remain unexploitable by bluffs.

Formula for the MDF

MDF = pot_size / (pot_size + bet_size)

Different type of ranges

Capped range

A capped range is a range that contains no (or very few) strong hands.

Polarized range

A polarized range is one constructed of very strong hands and very weak hands, nuts/bluffs. The most common place you'll see polarized ranges are in position, on the river.

Condensed range

A condensed range is a capped range that is also tight. This range does not contain the strongest hands, but is lean and contains many medium-strength hands. Also known as "depolarized".

Merged Ranges

Merged ranges are ones which have not been polarised either by a player's action or the board texture.

Merged ranges contain lots of medium strength hands at a high concentration but also some strong and weak hands.

A merged range is like a condensed range only more air heavy and less capped - it has not moved towards the middle, it simply contains a lot of middling hands naturally.
