Poker Theory: Betting

Value betting

- When you bet and your opponent calls, your equity goes down. You need to have at least 50% after you get called (finishing equity) to value bet the turn and 55%-60% to value bet the river.

- In game theory there are three different situations with respect to value betting.

  • You Cannot Value Bet
  • It's Optional to Value Bet
  • You Must Value Bet

- And there are two key factors that determine which of the three situations you are in:

  • Urgency
  • Landing Equity

You Must Value Bet

If your hand is strong enought to pick a certain bet size.

AND

If Urgency is high.

It's Optional to Value Bet

If your hand is strong enought to pick a certain bet size.

AND

If Urgency is not high.

You Cannot Value Bet

When your hand is not strong enough to pick any bet-size.

OR

You are not building a betting range using a sizing small enough to value bet your hand.

The Effect of Bet Sizing on Equity

The bigger you bet, the more your opponent will contract his range and the bigger the gap between your landing equity and finishing equity.

Required Landing Equity for Different Bet Sizes

We need to know roughly how much starting equity we'd need for each bet size for our hand to be able to value bet for that size.

Bet Size Minimum Landing Equity for Value Betting Wet Turn Minimum Landing Equity for Value Betting Dry Turn or River
B33 55% 65%-70%
B75 65% 75%-80%
B150 75% 85%-90%

- On wet turn spots where our hand is ahead more often than our equity suggests, we can value bet with lower landing equity than on dry turns or rivers where we are either way ahead or way behind.

- When in position we need a little more equity to value bet as we risk re-opening the action and therefore checking is a slightly more viable alternative in many cases.

- When out of position we need a little less equity to value bet as check is a slightly less viable alternative since we are not closing the action for that street by checking.

Urgency

Urgency refers to how imporant it is to value bet Now. It is dependant on the amount of time remaing in a hand to invest money compared with the average investment ceiling your hand wants to reach.

- Weaker Value Betting Hands can often delay their betting until later on in the hand since they don't need to invest so much by the end.

- Stronger Value Betting Hands want to invest more money by the end of the hand so they usually have to start making larger investments a bit earlier in the hand than weaker hands.

- At Shorter Stack Depths urgency is lower as there is less money to be invested by the end of the hand.

Denial (aka Procetion)

Equity denial is when you prevent a player from realizing his equity by forcing him to fold before showdown.

- Denying equity to live hands your opponent has to fold is a useful bonus but not a sufficient reason for betting.

- Protection comes in handy when the fold equity you get by betting is largely useful to you.

Reasons for Betting

Main reasons for betting are value and bluff. Equity denial is a useful bonus but not a sufficient reason for betting.
