Theorems

Containment Theorem

When you bet turn with a range full of semi-bluffs and the river bricks, you will be in a neutral-unfavourable spot with a range full of air. To refrain from overbluffing you must give up with a large quantity of your air. Such restraint is very difficult for a human.

Range Origin Theorem

The final result of a range is often more strongly dependant on its furthest back ancestor range than on any of the steps in between.

Positions Impact

Origin theorem tells us that the earlier positions are inherently less bluffed by the river than the later positions. Preflop ranges can have the starkest impact on imbalances by the river.

BU and SB tend to do a lot of overbluffing, whereas UTG and HJ trend towards underbluffing by the river.

The CO tends to be a neutral position where ranges are of a medium concentration of naturally strong and weak hands and where the average player is more likely to be balanced in the average spot.

The Most Overbluffed Node

The most extreme example of overbluffing is scattered aggression from fish who suddenly pot the river.

Beware "Making up for Lost Ground" Bets

"Making up for Lost Ground"-line is when weaker players starts betting big or overbetting after checking on the flop.

Scattergun Theorem

If a fish ever deploys scattered aggression against you, do not fold.

Unless the river sizing was a formidably sized overbet.
