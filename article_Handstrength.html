Poker Theory: Betting

Hand Strength and Polarization

Relative Hand Strength

- We have already seen how important it is to be able to estimate your equity when choosing between lines like betting and checking.

Now that we understand the functions of betting, it makes sense to try to become better judges of our hand's worth in a given spot to see if it qualifies for further investment.

To do this we must reject the absolute strength of our hand. EG. One pair, top pair, flush etc. and engage with the following factors which determine the true worth of our hand; or relative hand strength.

These factors can drastically affect the means and quantity of investment your hand wants to make.

Range Filtering

Range Filtering is the amount of contraction that has taken place in a range. When a range contracts (sheds some of its combos) it can do so in two different ways.

Polarising - A range contracts by polarising when it removes some of its more middling hands by taking a more aggressive action than it could have.

Condensing - A range contracts by condensing when it removes some of its strongest and weakest hands by taking a more passive action than it could have.

Relative Hand Strength vs Polarisation

When your opponent has polarised to a significant extent a few things will happen to your hand:

  • It will become harder to value bet thinly and you may need to check some quite good looking hands since Villain has removed the more middling hands which could normally call and lose.
  • You will want to defend passively to aggression - meaning by calling rather than raising in most cases. This is because Villain's range is contracting towards either hands that cannot call a further investment by you or hands that are so strong that they will beat you; or if you are ahead, then these hands are likely to keep investing later anyway if you just call.

Relative Hand Strength vs Condensing

When your opponent has condensed to a significant extent your approach will change in the following ways:

  • You should use larger sizing and seek value more aggressively since Villain is now capped and cannot harm your strong hands. There is no downside to large value bets if we have sufficient landing equity since we cannot isolate ourselves against too strong of a range.
  • You should avoid slowplaying for the most part since Villain is skewed towards the middle of his range and such hands do most of their investing by calling not by betting.

Merged Ranges

Merged ranges are ones which have not been polarised either by a player's action or the board texture.

Merged ranges contain lots of medium strength hands at a high concentration but also some strong and weak hands.

A merged range is like a condensed range only more air heavy and less capped - it has not moved towards the middle, it simply contains a lot of middling hands naturally.

Cannon Rule

When you think your opponent's range is merged or condensed you can fire aggressively for value and as a bluff and deploy a large sizing. But as your opponent's range becomes more polar, your investment level as the bettor should drop.

Texture

The board texture massively affects our relative hand strength.

On wetter textures a rank of hand (e.g. one pair) will be weaker than on drier textures.

On wetter textures a an absolute rank of hand (e.g. one pair) will be weaker than on drier textures.

We can split textures into three broad categories:

Dry Textures

On Dry Textures, the BB callers' range will be more merged on low boards where he has a lot of mediocre pairs and more polar on higher boards where he "either got it or he doesn't"

We should therefore use larger flop sizing on lower flops and smaller sizing on bigger flops.

Semi-Wet Textures

Semi-wet textures leave Villain's range quite merged/condensed on both high and low flop textures. We can deploy a larger bet with strong hands and various bluffs on all semi-wet unpaired textures.

Wet Textures

On wet textures the BB caller becomes more polarised and we should prefer smaller bets.

Turn and River Texture

A turn or river texture is the combination of all 4 or all 5 cards. Do not focus too heavily on just the most recent one.

Our minimum absolute hand strength for value betting will change massively based on the way a texture runs out.

Turn Urgency

Our good hands on the flop will lose their urgency to double barrel the turn for value when the card is messy.

Configuration

The configuration is the range vs range match up that occurs based on the pre-flop positions and action sequence of the parties who go post-flop.

In tighter configurations relative hand strength will be below absolute hand strength.

In looser configurations relative hand strength will be above absolute hand strength.
