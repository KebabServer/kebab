Facing a Polarised Range

BU's range contains hardly any yellow hands. It is quite polarised. Clarke's theorem recommends a small bet.

Note that a big bet would cause a very stark splitting of BU's range. He would be able to just fold all orange range.

Facing a Merged Range

Villain's range is completely merged here. Most hands are yellow.

Clarke's theorem recommends mainly big bet or check/raise (both large investments) because our range is more polar than Villain's.

Low Dry Theorem

When the flop is low and dry in a 3-Bet pot, we can usually simplify to just three plans:

  • Check/Raise
  • B60
  • Check/Fold

Let's look at the SB's C-Bet strategy and SB's strategy after checking and facing B33.

There's no EV loss playing raise or fold only against GTO opponent.

Beware the Dreaded Queen

In the Carrot Corner's ranges BU has a far greater concentration of Qx than any other broadway, mainly due to off-suit combos being so adundant and unique to Qx.

Beware absolutism here. It might be true that SB has more total combos of Qx, but what matters is their concentration within a range.

The Friendly Broadways

For this very reason, the King, Jack and Ten are very good broadways for SB's range.
